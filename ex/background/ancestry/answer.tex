A (partial) graph of the ancestry of the Simpsons could be constructed like this:

\vspace{10mm}
\begin{center}
  \begin{tikzpicture}[remember picture]
    \newcommand{\xspacing}[0]{2.5cm}
    \newcommand{\yspacing}[0]{2.5cm}
    \newcommand{\labelshift}[0]{5cm}
    
    \tikzstyle{edge}  = [thick,>=stealth,draw=black]
    \tikzstyle{dedge} = [thick,->,>=stealth,draw=black]
    \tikzstyle{node}=[
      circle,
      draw=purple,
      anchor=north,
      align=center,
      thick,
      minimum size=1,
    ]
    
    \coordinate (origo) at (0, 0);
    \node[node] (AbeSimpson)       at ([xshift=0.0*\xspacing,yshift=-0*\yspacing] origo) {Abe\\Simpson};
    \node[node] (MonaSimpson)      at ([xshift=1.0*\xspacing,yshift=-0*\yspacing] origo) {Mona\\Simpson};
    \node[node] (ClancyBouver)     at ([xshift=2.5*\xspacing,yshift=-0*\yspacing] origo) {Clancy\\Bouver};
    \node[node] (JacquelineBouver) at ([xshift=3.5*\xspacing,yshift=-0*\yspacing] origo) {Jacqueline\\Bouver};
    
    \node[node] (HerbSimpson)  at ([xshift=0*\xspacing,yshift=-1*\yspacing] origo) {Herb\\Simpson};
    \node[node] (HomerSimpson) at ([xshift=1*\xspacing,yshift=-1*\yspacing] origo) {Homer\\Simpson};
    \node[node] (MargeBouver)  at ([xshift=2*\xspacing,yshift=-1*\yspacing] origo) {Marge\\Bouver};
    \node[node] (PattyBouver)  at ([xshift=3*\xspacing,yshift=-1*\yspacing] origo) {Patty\\Bouver};
    \node[node] (SelmaBouver)  at ([xshift=4*\xspacing,yshift=-1*\yspacing] origo) {Selma\\Bouver};
    
    \node[node] (BartSimpson)   at ([xshift=0.5*\xspacing,yshift=-2*\yspacing] origo) {Bart\\Simpson};
    \node[node] (LisaSimpson)   at ([xshift=1.5*\xspacing,yshift=-2*\yspacing] origo) {Lisa\\Simpson};
    \node[node] (MaggieSimpson) at ([xshift=2.5*\xspacing,yshift=-2*\yspacing] origo) {Maggie\\Simpson};
    \node[node] (LingBouver)    at ([xshift=4.0*\xspacing,yshift=-2*\yspacing] origo) {Ling\\Bouver};
    
    \draw[dedge] (AbeSimpson)--(HerbSimpson);
    \draw[dedge] (AbeSimpson)--(HomerSimpson);
    \draw[dedge] (MonaSimpson)--(HerbSimpson);
    \draw[dedge] (MonaSimpson)--(HomerSimpson);
    \draw[dedge] (ClancyBouver)--(MargeBouver);
    \draw[dedge] (ClancyBouver)--(PattyBouver);
    \draw[dedge] (ClancyBouver)--(SelmaBouver);
    \draw[dedge] (JacquelineBouver)--(MargeBouver);
    \draw[dedge] (JacquelineBouver)--(PattyBouver);
    \draw[dedge] (JacquelineBouver)--(SelmaBouver);
    \draw[dedge] (HomerSimpson)--(BartSimpson);
    \draw[dedge] (HomerSimpson)--(LisaSimpson);
    \draw[dedge] (HomerSimpson)--(MaggieSimpson);
    \draw[dedge] (MargeBouver)--(BartSimpson);
    \draw[dedge] (MargeBouver)--(LisaSimpson);
    \draw[dedge] (MargeBouver)--(MaggieSimpson);
    \draw[dedge] (SelmaBouver)--(LingBouver);
  \end{tikzpicture}
\end{center}
\vspace{10mm}

In this example, the following graph-related terms are in use:
\begin{itemize}
  \item A node is used to represent a person.
  \item An edge is used to represent an parent-child relation. That is, it goes from a parent to a child.
\end{itemize}
